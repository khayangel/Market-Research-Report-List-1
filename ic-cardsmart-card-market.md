<p><p>IC cards, also known as smart cards, are used for storing and transmitting data securely. In the contact market, these cards require physical contact with a card reader to exchange information. This type is commonly used for bank cards and identification documents. Conversely, contactless IC cards use radio frequency identification (RFID) technology to communicate wirelessly with a reader, without the need for direct contact. These cards are frequently utilized in public transportation systems and access control, offering convenience and speed for users.</p></p>
