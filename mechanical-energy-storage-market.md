<p><p>Mechanical energy storage refers to the utilization of mechanical systems to store and release energy. One type is Pumped Hydro Storage (PHS), where surplus energy is used to pump water from a lower reservoir to a higher reservoir, and when energy is needed, the water flows back down, turning turbines and generating electricity. Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES) works by storing compressed air in underground caverns and releasing it to generate electricity when required. Other types of mechanical energy storage include flywheels and springs, which store energy in rotational or potential form for later use.</p></p>
