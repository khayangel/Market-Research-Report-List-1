<p><p>Antimicrobial food packaging additives serve as a protective barrier against the growth of microorganisms on food products, preventing contamination and extending shelf life. There are various types of additives used for this purpose. Organic acids, such as benzoic acid and sorbic acid, have antimicrobial properties and are commonly used. Enzymes work by breaking down the cell walls of microorganisms. Fungicides are substances that inhibit the growth of fungi. Essential oils, like thyme or oregano oil, possess antimicrobial properties. Other additives may include substances like silver or copper ions, which have antimicrobial effects.</p></p>
