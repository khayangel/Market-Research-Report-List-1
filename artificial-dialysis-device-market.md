<p><p>Artificial Dialysis Devices are used to replace kidney function in patients with end-stage renal disease. There are two main types of dialysis devices in the market: Hemodialysis and Peritoneal Dialysis. Hemodialysis involves the use of a machine to filter blood outside the body, while Peritoneal Dialysis uses the patient's peritoneum as a filter. Both types of dialysis have their own advantages and disadvantages, providing options for patients depending on their individual needs and preferences.</p></p>
