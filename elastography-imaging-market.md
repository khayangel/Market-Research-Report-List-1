<p><p>Elastography imaging is a medical technique used to assess tissue stiffness, which can help identify abnormal conditions like cancer, liver fibrosis, and cardiovascular diseases. There are different types of elastography imaging, including ultrasound elastography and magnetic resonance elastography (MRE). Ultrasound elastography utilizes sound waves to measure tissue elasticity, providing real-time imaging. MRE, on the other hand, uses magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) to generate images and quantify tissue stiffness. Both techniques offer non-invasive alternatives to traditional biopsies for diagnosing and monitoring various diseases.</p></p>
