Perpendicular parking involves vehicles parked perpendicular to the curb, also known as nose-in parking. Parallel parking requires parking the vehicle parallel to the curb, typically between two parked vehicles. These different market types cater to different parking space configurations and help optimize space utilization, traffic flow, and overall parking management.</p></p>
