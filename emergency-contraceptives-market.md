<p><p>The emergency contraceptives market includes various types of pills. Combined estrogen and progestin pills, like Plan B One-Step, contain synthetic versions of hormones to prevent pregnancy. Progestin-only pills, such as levonorgestrel or Plan B, work to inhibit or delay ovulation. Antiprogestin pills, like mifepristone, block progesterone receptors. Ulipristal acetate pills, like Ella, are selective progesterone receptor modulators that prevent pregnancy by delaying or inhibiting ovulation. These different types of emergency contraceptives provide women with options for preventing unintended pregnancies after unprotected sex.</p></p>
