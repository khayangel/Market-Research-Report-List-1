<p><strong>The Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Heat Flux DSC</li><li>Power Compensated DSC</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC) is a technique used to measure thermal events in materials. Heat Flux DSC is a type of DSC that measures the heat flow between a sample and a reference material. It is commonly used for studying phase transitions, such as melting and crystallization. Power Compensated DSC, on the other hand, maintains a constant temperature difference between the sample and reference by adjusting the power input. This type of DSC is suitable for analyzing samples with complex or variable thermal behavior. Both market types provide valuable insights into material properties.</p></p>
