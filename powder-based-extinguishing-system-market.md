<p><p>A powder-based extinguishing system is a type of fire suppression system that utilizes powdered extinguishing agents to suppress or extinguish fires. This is primarily attributed to the increasing demand for effective fire protection systems across various end-use industries such as oil and gas, manufacturing, automotive, and aerospace. Additionally, stringent fire safety regulations imposed by government authorities are also driving the adoption of these systems.</p><p>Powder-based extinguishing systems offer several advantages over other types of fire suppression systems. They are effective in extinguishing different types of fires, including Class A (ordinary combustibles), Class B (flammable liquids), and Class C (electrical) fires. These systems are also non-conductive and do not leave any residue, making them suitable for use in electrical and electronic equipment environments.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the powder-based extinguishing system market is the integration of advanced technologies such as automation and remote monitoring.
