<p><p>Monoblock amplifiers are categorized into different market types based on their amplification technology. Class A amplifiers are known for their high-quality sound reproduction but are less efficient as they operate in full power regardless of the signal level. Class B amplifiers are more efficient but suffer from crossover distortion due to splitting signals between two transistors. Class A/B amplifiers aim to combine benefits of both Class A and B technologies. Class D amplifiers use pulse width modulation to amplify signals efficiently. Other market types may include Class G, H, T, and digital amplifiers, each with its own unique characteristics and applications.</p></p>
