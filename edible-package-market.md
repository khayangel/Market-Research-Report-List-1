<p><p>The edible packaging market encompasses various types based on its composition. Lipids are fats or oils that can be used to form films. Polysaccharides refer to carbohydrates which can create a barrier. Proteins have the ability to form films as well. Surfactants are compounds that lower surface tension and can be used to stabilize packaging films. Composite films are a combination of different materials, such as lipids, polysaccharides, proteins, and surfactants, providing enhanced packaging properties. These types offer alternative and sustainable solutions for packaging in various industries.</p></p>
