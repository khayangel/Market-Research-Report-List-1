<p><p>Sphenoidal electrodes are specific types of electrodes used for intracranial electroencephalography (EEG) recordings in individuals with drug-resistant epilepsy. These electrodes are placed within the sphenoidal sinus, a hollow cavity within the sphenoid bone located in close proximity to the brain. Sphenoidal electrodes are inserted through the nasal passage to provide direct access to the targeted brain regions for recording brain activity.</p><p>The sphenoidal electrodes market is experiencing significant growth due to the rising prevalence of epilepsy and increasing demand for accurate and effective diagnostic tools. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.5% during the forecast period.</p><p>One of the key factors driving the market growth is the increasing adoption of minimally invasive surgical procedures. Sphenoidal electrodes offer a less invasive alternative to traditional intracranial electrodes, reducing the risk of complications and improving patient comfort.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in electrode technology, such as the development of flexible and biocompatible materials, are expected to drive the market growth. These advancements enhance the electrode's durability and flexibility, allowing for better contact with brain tissue and improving the accuracy of EEG readings.</p><p>Another trend in the sphenoidal electrodes market is the integration of electrode technology with advanced imaging techniques.
