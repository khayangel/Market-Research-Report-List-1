<p><p>Propulsion systems in the market can be categorized into two types: air breathing engines and non-air breathing engines. Air breathing engines rely on oxygen from the atmosphere to burn fuel and generate thrust. These engines are commonly found in commercial airplanes and are further classified into jet engines and turboprop. Non-air breathing engines, on the other hand, do not require atmospheric oxygen for combustion. These engines, like rocket engines, carry their own oxidizer and fuel, making them suitable for space exploration and military applications.</p></p>
