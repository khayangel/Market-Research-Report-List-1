<p><p>Pea protein is a type of plant-based protein derived from yellow peas, also known as Pisum sativum. It is made by isolating the protein from the peas, removing the starch and fiber, and then drying it into a powder form. Pea protein is known for its high nutritional value, as it contains all nine essential amino acids required by the human body.</p><p>The market for pea protein has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by the increasing demand for plant-based protein alternatives. This growth can be attributed to various factors such as the rising preference for vegan and vegetarian diets, the increasing health consciousness among consumers, and the growing population of individuals with lactose intolerance or allergies to other sources of protein like soy and dairy.</p><p>Additionally, the pea protein market has been positively impacted by the expanding usage of plant-based protein in sports nutrition products, dietary supplements, and functional food and beverages. Pea protein is often used as an ingredient in protein powders, bars, and beverages due to its excellent amino acid profile and ability to promote muscle recovery and growth.</p><p>In terms of market trends, there is a growing focus on product innovation and development to cater to diverse consumer preferences.
