<p><p>Mucopolysaccharidosis (MPS) is a rare genetic disorder that affects the body's ability to break down complex carbohydrates. This results in the build-up of glycosaminoglycans (GAGs) in various tissues and organs, leading to a wide range of symptoms and complications. Although there is currently no cure for MPS, various treatment options are available to manage the symptoms and improve the quality of life for affected individuals.</p><p>The mainstay of treatment for MPS involves enzyme replacement therapy (ERT), which involves intravenous infusion of the missing enzyme to help break down the accumulated GAGs. ERT has been shown to significantly improve various symptoms such as organomegaly, skeletal abnormalities, and respiratory dysfunction. However, its efficacy varies depending on the specific MPS type and disease severity.</p><p>In addition to ERT, other treatment approaches for MPS include hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT), substrate reduction therapy, and gene therapy. HSCT can be curative for certain MPS types by replacing the patient's defective cells with healthy donor cells. Substrate reduction therapy aims to decrease the production of GAGs in the body, while gene therapy focuses on correcting the underlying genetic defect responsible for MPS.</p><p>The Mucopolysaccharidosis (MPS) Treatment Market has been experiencing significant growth, primarily driven by advancements in treatment options and increased awareness and diagnosis of rare genetic disorders.
