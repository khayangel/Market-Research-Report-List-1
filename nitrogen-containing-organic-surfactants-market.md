<p><p>The nitrogen-containing organic surfactants market can be categorized into three types: amine salt, quaternary ammonium salt, and others. Amine salt surfactants are derived from amines and exhibit excellent emulsifying and foaming properties. Quaternary ammonium salt surfactants are composed of positively charged ions and are recognized for their strong cleaning abilities and antimicrobial properties. Lastly, the "others" category refers to surfactants that contain nitrogen but do not fall under the amine salt or quaternary ammonium salt classification, such as imidazoline-based surfactants. These surfactants offer a variety of functional properties suitable for various applications.</p></p>
