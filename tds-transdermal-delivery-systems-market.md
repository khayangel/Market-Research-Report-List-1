<p><p>Transdermal Delivery Systems (TDS) are ways of delivering drugs through the skin for systemic distribution. The market for TDS is categorized into three types: Single-layer Drug-in-Adhesive, Multi-layer Drug-in-Adhesive, and Others. Single-layer Drug-in-Adhesive refers to patches consisting of a single layer of drug-containing adhesive, providing controlled drug release. Multi-layer Drug-in-Adhesive patches contain multiple layers, allowing for complex drug release profiles. The Others market includes alternative TDS technologies like reservoir systems or matrix-based patches. These different types cater to varying drug release needs, offering flexibility and diversity in transdermal drug delivery options.</p></p>
