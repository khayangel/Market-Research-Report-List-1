<p><ul><li>Allogeneic</li><li>Autologous</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Regenerative cell market is divided into two main types: allogeneic and autologous. Allogeneic regenerative cells are derived from a donor and can be used in multiple patients. These cells are carefully selected, processed, and stored for therapeutic purposes. On the other hand, autologous regenerative cells are derived from the patient's own body. These cells are collected, processed, and then reintroduced into the patient. Both types have their own advantages and limitations, and their selection depends on factors such as availability, compatibility, and effectiveness for specific medical conditions.</p></p>
