<p><ul><li>Relational Database</li><li>Non-relational Database</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The enterprise database market consists of two main types: relational databases and non-relational databases. Relational databases organize data in tables with predefined relationships between them, ensuring data integrity and supporting complex queries. On the other hand, non-relational databases store data in a more flexible and unstructured manner, enabling scalability and high performance for big data applications. Both types serve different needs, with relational databases focusing on structured data and non-relational databases addressing the challenges of storing and processing large volumes of unstructured or semi-structured data.</p></p>
