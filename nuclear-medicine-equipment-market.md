<p><p>Nuclear medicine equipment refers to technology used in the diagnosis, monitoring, and treatment of various medical conditions using radioactive substances. The market for nuclear medicine equipment can be divided into two main types: hybrid SPECT and standalone SPECT. Hybrid SPECT combines the features of single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT) and computed tomography (CT) in one machine, allowing for more accurate imaging and improved disease detection. Standalone SPECT, on the other hand, is a dedicated imaging system that solely performs SPECT scans, providing detailed functional information about the organs and tissues being examined.</p></p>
